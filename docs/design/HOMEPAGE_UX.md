# Homepage UX

The homepage is not a marketing landing page. It is the beginning of the working product.

The parent should immediately understand:

1. Vika helps them see what happened differently.
2. They can start by describing what they saw.
3. They can choose immediate support or pattern understanding.

## Current Homepage Flow

1. Wordmark: `Vika Calm`
2. Hero: `Small moments of understanding create lasting change.`
3. Intro: `Describe what you saw. Together, we'll explore what happened before deciding how to respond.`
4. Two pathway cards:
   - `Guide me`
   - `Help me understand`
5. Coaching input
6. Guiding principle: `Understanding comes before responding.`
7. Recent conversations
8. Vika framework

This flow should keep the parent moving toward action without rushing them past observation.

## Stressed Parent Review

When reviewing the homepage, imagine a parent opening it after a difficult morning.

Check:

- Is there too much text before the parent can act?
- Is the next action obvious?
- Does the page sound calm without being patronizing?
- Does it avoid blame?
- Does it invite curiosity?
- Does it avoid generic AI language?
- Does it avoid feeling like a dashboard?
- Is mobile use comfortable?

## Homepage Copy Rules

Keep the homepage oriented around observation:

- `Describe what you saw.`
- `what happened`
- `what you noticed`
- `before deciding how to respond`

Avoid:

- `find the why`
- `tell us the behavior`
- `parenting advice`
- `you are not failing`
- `don't worry`

## Recent Conversations

Recent conversations are useful but secondary.

They should not compete with the main coaching entry. They should feel like a quiet continuation point, not a dashboard module.

## VIKA Framework Section

Keep this section simple and editorial.

Current copy:

- Validate: `Help your child feel safe and understood.`
- Investigate: `Look at what happened before, during and after.`
- Know: `Consider the need, communication or missing skill beneath what happened.`
- Act: `Choose one calm next step.`

Do not over-design it with icons or heavy cards.
